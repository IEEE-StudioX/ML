Cosine Similarity Explanation

Cosine Similarity is a metric used to measure the similarity between two vectors.
It is often used in recommendation systems, text mining, and clustering tasks.
Cosine similarity calculates the cosine of the angle between two non-zero vectors in a multi-dimensional space. The result is a value between 0 and 1, where:

  • 1 indicates that the two vectors are identical (high similarity).
  • 0 indicates that the vectors are orthogonal, meaning there is no similarity.
